<p><p>The Alexandrite Rod market features two primary manufacturing methods: the Czochralski method and the Float-Zone method. The Czochralski method involves melting the material and slowly pulling a seed crystal to create a single crystal structure, ensuring high purity and uniformity. Conversely, the Float-Zone method involves melting a rod of material by passing it through an induction coil, allowing for minimal contamination and enhanced crystal quality. Both methods cater to various applications in jewelry and optical devices, each offering distinct advantages in terms of purity and structural integrity.</p></p>
