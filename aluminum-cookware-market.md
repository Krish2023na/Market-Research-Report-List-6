<p><p>The aluminum cookware market features various types, primarily cast aluminum and anodized aluminum cookware. Cast aluminum cookware is known for its excellent heat distribution and durability, making it ideal for frying and baking. Its heavier construction contributes to even cooking. Anodized aluminum cookware undergoes an electrochemical process that enhances its resistance to scratching and corrosion, providing a non-reactive surface for cooking. This type is lighter and often features a non-stick coating, offering a versatile and easy-to-clean option for modern kitchens.</p></p>
