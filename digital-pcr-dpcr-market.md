<p><p>Digital PCR (dPCR) is a highly sensitive method for nucleic acid quantification that divides a sample into numerous individual reactions, allowing for precise measurement of target molecules. Digital Microfluidic Chip PCR and Droplet Digital PCR are two main types of dPCR technology. Digital Microfluidic Chip PCR uses miniaturized devices to manipulate and analyze small volumes of samples, while Droplet Digital PCR partitions samples into thousands of droplets for individual PCR reactions. Both methods offer high sensitivity and accuracy for various applications in research and diagnostics.</p></p>
