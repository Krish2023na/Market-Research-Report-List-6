<p><p>Electromechanical blood flow meters utilize varying waveforms to measure blood flow dynamics. Sine wave meters provide smooth, continuous signals, ideal for accurately assessing blood velocity and volume, aligning well with physiological conditions. In contrast, square wave meters generate abrupt changes, offering sharper measurements but may introduce artifacts in certain scenarios. Both types cater to specific clinical and research needs, influencing their market presence. The choice between sine and square wave meters can impact measurement precision and application suitability in various healthcare settings.</p></p>
