<p><p>Glass ionomer cement (GIC) for cementations typically comes in three main types: powder, liquid, and set. The powder form consists of aluminosilicate glass that, when mixed with a liquid, forms the paste used for dental applications. The liquid component is usually a polyacrylic acid solution, which, when combined with the powder, activates the setting process. Once mixed, the set form refers to the hardened material that bonds to both tooth structure and restorative materials, providing effective dental adhesion and protection.</p></p>
