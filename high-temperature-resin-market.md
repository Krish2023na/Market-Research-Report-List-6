<p><ul><li>Phenolic</li><li>Epoxy</li><li>Thermoplastic</li><li>Polyimide</li><li>Benzoxazine</li><li>Cyanate Ester</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The high-temperature resin market comprises several key types, each suited for specific applications. Phenolic resins offer excellent thermal stability and flame resistance, making them ideal for aerospace and automotive sectors. Epoxy resins are known for their exceptional adhesion and chemical resistance. Thermoplastic resins provide flexibility and ease of processing. Polyimide resins excel in extreme temperature environments. Benzoxazine resins combine the benefits of thermosets and thermoplastics, while cyanate esters offer low moisture absorption and superior electrical properties, catering to electronics and aerospace applications.</p></p>
