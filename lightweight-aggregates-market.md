<p><p>The lightweight aggregates market consists of various types, including expansive clay, expanded shale, sintered fly ash, and others. Expansive clay is derived from clay minerals that expand when heated, providing low density and enhanced insulation. Expanded shale is produced by heating crushed shale, resulting in porous aggregates ideal for concrete applications. Sintered fly ash, made from recycled industrial ash, offers sustainability and lightweight properties. Other aggregates encompass diverse materials like pumice and scoria, each contributing unique benefits in construction and building projects.</p></p>
