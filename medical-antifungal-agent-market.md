<p><p>The medical antifungal agent market is divided into five main types: Azoles, Echinocandins, Polyenes, Allylamines, and Others. Azoles are the most commonly used antifungal drugs and work by inhibiting the synthesis of ergosterol in fungal cells. Echinocandins work by inhibiting the synthesis of beta-glucan in the fungal cell wall. Polyenes disrupt the cell membrane of fungi. Allylamines inhibit the synthesis of ergosterol. Other types of antifungal agents include nucleoside analogs, flucytosine, and griseofulvin.</p></p>
