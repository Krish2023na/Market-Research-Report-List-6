<p><p>Natural polymers are divided into four main types in the market:</p><p>1. Cellulose ethers: These are derived from cellulose, a natural polymer found in plants, and are used in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, food, and cosmetics.</p><p>2. Exudate & vegetable gums: These are natural polymers obtained from plant exudates or seeds, and are commonly used as thickening agents in food products.</p><p>3. Starch & fermentation products: Starch is a natural polymer found in plants, while fermentation products are produced through microbial processes. Both are used in various industries, including food, pharmaceuticals, and textiles.</p><p>4. Other polymers: This category includes a wide range of natural polymers such as proteins, chitin, and collagen, which have diverse applications in different industries.</p></p>
