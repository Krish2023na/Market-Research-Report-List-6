<p><p>The near-beer market encompasses beverages with very low alcohol content, typically less than 0.5% ABV. It primarily includes two types: limit fermentation and dealcoholization methods. Limit fermentation involves reducing the fermentation process to keep alcohol levels low while retaining some beer characteristics. In contrast, dealcoholization methods remove alcohol from fully fermented beer, preserving flavors while creating a non-alcoholic drink. Both approaches cater to consumers seeking alternatives to traditional alcoholic beverages, driven by health and wellness trends.</p></p>
