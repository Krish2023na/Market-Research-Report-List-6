<p><p>Non-contact magnetostrictive position sensors are categorized into two primary types: analog and digital. Analog types provide continuous output signals that represent the position of an object, offering high precision and resolution for various applications. In contrast, digital types deliver discrete signals, typically using binary coding, which simplifies integration with digital systems and enhances reliability. Both types utilize magnetostrictive principles, allowing them to accurately measure position without physical contact, making them ideal for harsh environments and applications requiring durability.</p></p>
