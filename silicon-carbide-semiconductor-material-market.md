<p><p>Silicon Carbide (SiC) semiconductor materials are categorized primarily into four main types based on their crystallographic structures. </p><p>- **2H-SiC** features a hexagonal structure, ideal for certain high-temperature applications.</p><p>- **3C-SiC** has a cubic crystal structure, offering good electron mobility and is suitable for electronic devices.</p><p>- **4H-SiC** combines high thermal conductivity with a wide bandgap, making it popular for power devices.</p><p>- **6H-SiC**, another hexagonal structure, is used in high-power and high-temperature applications. </p><p>"Others" may include variations or lesser-known types utilized in niche applications.</p></p>
