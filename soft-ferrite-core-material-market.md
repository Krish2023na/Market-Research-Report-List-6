<p><p>Soft ferrite core materials are classified into three types based on their compositions: nickel-zinc ferrite core, manganese-zinc ferrite core, and magnesium-zinc ferrite core. Nickel-zinc ferrite cores have high permeability and low losses, making them suitable for high-frequency applications. Manganese-zinc ferrite cores offer good temperature stability and are commonly used in power applications. Magnesium-zinc ferrite cores are known for their low cost and low losses, making them ideal for high-volume consumer electronics. Each type of ferrite core material has unique characteristics that make them suitable for specific applications.</p></p>
