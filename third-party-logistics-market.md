<p><p>Third-Party Logistics (3PL) markets can be categorized into three types: B2B, B2C, and C2C. In B2B (Business-to-Business), 3PL providers manage logistics for companies, enhancing supply chain efficiency. B2C (Business-to-Consumer) involves logistics services that support businesses delivering products directly to end customers, focusing on speed and customer satisfaction. C2C (Consumer-to-Consumer) logistics facilitate transactions between individual consumers, often using platforms that streamline shipping and delivery processes, creating a seamless experience for peer-to-peer sales and exchanges.</p></p>
