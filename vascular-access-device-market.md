<p><strong>The Vascular Access Device Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Central Vascular Access Devices</li><li>Peripheral Vascular Access Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The vascular access device market is categorized into two main types: central vascular access devices (CVADs) and peripheral vascular access devices (PVADs). CVADs, such as central catheters and implanted ports, are designed for long-term use, allowing access to large veins for treatments like chemotherapy and dialysis. In contrast, PVADs, including peripheral intravenous catheters, are used for short-term access to smaller veins for procedures like medication administration and fluid delivery. Both types are essential for effective patient care in medical settings.</p></p>
